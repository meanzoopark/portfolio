Curators﹒UX/UI Design

CuratorsUX/UI Design

People seek out "news," an information tool that organizes and presents various facets of society across diverse themes, for several reasons: 1. To acquire information directly relevant to their lives through the news 2. To feel a sense of centrality through the news 3. Due to the human desire for an endless supply of new content.
In the present day, social media has shifted towards a multiple-way communication model, departing entirely from the one-sided structure. Whether it's one-to-one, many-to-many, or many-to-one, individuals express their thoughts and emotions (beyond the confines of a personal diary), although the impact on others remains uncertain.
News has historically served as a platform for one-way communication. While the composition of news is contingent on the conscience of the writers or editors-in-chief, only content from individuals or organizations officially certified through an authentication process can be deemed as news. Users express their sentiments passively since they cannot alter the content itself. Additionally, users often share links to articles that align with their thoughts and values on separate platforms. It is essential to note that news is not synonymous with an SNS platform.
The world is marked by various political and cultural conflicts. While everyone has the right to hold their opinions corresponding with their values, unproductive debates persist due to differences in ideas. To foster healthier and more productive conversations, there is a growing need for a platform that amalgamates the truthfulness and informativeness of news, and the openness and lightness of SNS. News' essential characteristics remain unaltered. However, through the [Curators], individuals can unveil, directly share, and express their values in diverse ways.
[Curators] embody a persona disenchanted with stagnant news platforms. This individual perceives the outdated and cluttered interface as a problem and emphasizes the importance of balancing factual accuracy and social engagement. Recognizing the potential risk of information bubbles, the persona laments the scarcity of original content. They anticipate real-time updates to align with the rapidly changing world and are cautious of time-consuming customization for modern individuals valuing their time. These concerns can be alleviated by the new platform, featuring clarity, simplicity, truthfulness, authenticity, balanced information, real-time updates, and convenient customization. This encapsulates the core goal of Curators, necessitating a trendy interface and a balance between producing trustworthy and balanced information.
Through three keywords,[Curators] pursue their goals. Firstly, they prioritize the sight of the users, capturing attention with strong branding color and infusing softness and fun into hand gestures based on user behavior. The platform encourages users to linger through interactions that go beyond a simple touch. Secondly, personalization. [Curators] curation boards reach users through personalized content. From the composition of the board to the title, layout, and overall mood, customized curation boards become a new communication tool, turning users into curators. Lastly, socializing and expressing are the ultimate goals of Curators. Boards comprised of clipped articles serve as a condensed medium for users to introduce and store their expressions. Active sharing and recommendation of articles and boards with neighbors are anticipated, transforming the platform into a medium for communication that goes beyond mere acceptance and expression.

Cognito Spark﹒UX/UI Design

Cognito SparkUX/UI Design

Finding the information you need quickly and easily can be a challenge in today's world of overflowing data. Search engines play a crucial role in helping us navigate this information overload, but they can still fall short in terms of understanding user intentions and ensuring information accuracy. Additionally, the rise of communication between humans and computers has created a demand for interactive assistants that can go beyond simple search functions.
Through extensive research and analysis, five fundamental categories were identified to underpin search engine functionality: relevance and accuracy, information quality, user experience, personalization, and privacy and security. Before users' direct engagement, usability considerations were thoroughly addressed, mapping out where and how functions would be integrated into the user interface. The goal was to evoke users' emotions and maintain their engagement at its peak, leading to the meticulous design of the search engine's structure.
During the development phase, these five foundational categories were strategically positioned within the structural configuration, providing a visual representation of their interaction in the design process. Anticipating how these trends would manifest in real-world user scenarios, wireframes were crafted detailing the service's provision. Finally, meticulous user interface development finalized the design.
The service was named "Cognito Spark." "Cognito" derives from Latin, meaning "Knowing," symbolizing the service's comprehensive search engine capabilities, while "Spark" denotes the AI Search Assistant. The name encapsulates the vision of instilling the joy of knowledge discovery and fostering excitement through interactions with Spark.
Cognito's Crawling system was designed to periodically select trending themes for indexing, ensuring the freshness of information. Through query modification, it adeptly identifies users' language preferences to minimize search time. The Ranking Algorithm prioritizes pre-selected information based on user behavior metrics and feedback, enhancing personalization. However, the importance of user privacy is recognized, so user-controlled settings were implemented to manage advertising services that leverage user data, assuring transparency and user autonomy.
Cognito Spark offers personalized information and enhances user experience by efficiently addressing typographical errors, clarifying queries, and organizing complex information through intuitive filters. Unlike traditional AI chatbots, Cognito Spark evolves through direct and indirect user feedback, striving to become the ultimate assistant and partner in users' search journeys.

sketch 181219_a﹒Book Design

sketch 181219_aBook Design

Computational thinking aids in defining abstract and ambiguous human thoughts and actions. Through the integration of this thinking into computational design, individuals can appreciate unexpected beauty beyond intentional elements within meticulously calculated formulas. This book, ironically transferred from the digital world to the refined analog medium of a book, traces the trends in computational thinking design from its illustrious beginnings. It also guides readers on incorporating their ideas into computational design, bridging the gap between the digital and analog realms.
The cover of the book is composed to showcase elements as they would appear through the eyes of a computer, using the programming tool Processing. This design serves as a small experiment, revealing the purpose of the design even before reaching the final result. The book spine is adorned with numbers, reminiscent of those displayed on the left side of programming tools, effectively conveying the essence the book signifies, surpassing even the direct title. This concept extends to the inside of a book and functions like a bookmark to help users find or display the sections they want.
For the fonts, the Noto Sans Mono Regular is used. A monospaced font is commonly used in programming, ensuring equal horizontal spacing for each character. The font's fixed width preserves the unique characteristics of each letter. Adjustments in line spacing and kerning were made to elevate readability, striking a balance between aesthetics and legibility. This adds an element of enjoyment to the book. The printing concept aligns with the theme of "computational" by using thin receipt-like paper. Additionally, RGB printing was chosen to mirror the appearance of inserted images on a monitor, emphasizing the book's exploration of computational thinking outcomes.
The title, "sketch181219a," signifies the initiation of the book on December 19, 2018, with "a" denoting the initial version of the sketch. Reflecting the book's distinctive nature, the code used to create the cover was adopted as the title, providing a clear starting point for the book's creation.
The book is organized into sections like "About Computational Design," "Computational Plus Design Equal," "Mapping the Beginnings of Computer-generated Art in the Netherlands," "DEMO-SCENE," "Interactive Graphic," "Idea," and "Animate Rules." Each section's design varies according to the character of the content. The introduction of computational design redefines the definition of design, starting with the visual announcement of the origins of computational design through generated art in the Netherlands. If the start was public interest and broad, the computational design through DEMO-SCENE later added a private and intensive character due to the home computer revolution of the 1970s. Subsequently, it engaged in mutual interaction with people, evolving into a unified medium encompassing individuals and communities through forms like interactive graphics, exemplified by designers Daito Manabe and Alva Noto. Insights into the creative ideas behind computational design are provided through Yugo Nakamura and Daniel Rozin. Finally, the book ends by vividly introducing Oskar Fischinger's work, which incorporates computational design into video media, as if watching a movie conference through the layout of the book.
